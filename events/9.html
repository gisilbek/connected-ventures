About This Event

On the 29th of August, Connected.Ventures co-organised the roundtable "Building Smarter Tech Ecosystems," where leading entrepreneurial ecosystem builders discussed what's working in our current innovation landscape, what gaps need addressing, and what strategic priorities should guide the UK's tech innovation playbook through 2030.

Co-organised with Startup Genome, Centre for Entrepreneurs, and The Entrepreneurs Network, and also hosted by London & Partners, the discussion brought together expertise spanning ecosystem building, technology development, and policy design.

Some insights from the keynote speakers:

Matt Smith, Vice President at Global Entrepreneurship Network, highlighted the UK's first-mover advantage in technology policy, from pioneering fintech regulation to developing tax incentives that now influence innovation frameworks globally, while emphasising the depth of specialised support spanning blockchain development to quantum computing applications.

Tim Barnes, CEO at Centre for Entrepreneurs, delivered compelling insights on evidence-based ecosystem development, revealing that the UK's 800+ support organisations represent one of the world's densest innovation infrastructures, and challenged conventional thinking about scaling tech ventures through sophisticated data analysis.

Natalia Loza, founder of Connected.Ventures and exited technology founder, contributed tech insights on digital infrastructure requirements for ecosystem coordination, analysing how computational resource allocation and data accessibility initiatives can optimise connectivity between universities, accelerators, and tech ventures and better support deep tech commercialisation pathways.

Vinous Ali, Deputy Executive Director at Startup Coalition, explored regulatory opportunities for emerging technologies, discussing how nimble policy frameworks could position the UK as a preferred testbed for e.g., autonomous vehicles, digital health solutions, and fintech applications.

Dr Christopher Haley, Head of Research at Startup Genome, provided international perspective on innovation ecosystems, examining how the UK can leverage its strengths in tech infrastructure development and resources to maintain competitive advantage in the global landscape.

The event culminated with a networking session bringing together entrepreneurial ecosystem builders from leading innovation organisations including Techstars, Zinc, Conception X, Carbon13, City of London Corporation, Imperial Innovation, the Commonwealth Secretariat, and Innovate UK's Catapult Network, among others.
